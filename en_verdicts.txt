## Task 2 вАФ Parallel linking

### What this means
A "parallel triplet" is the same semantic concept expressed in all three languages. For example, a sentence about a female engineer being underestimated would appear as:
- EN-G-001 (English version)
- FR-G-001 (French version, same concept)
- BG-G-001 (Bulgarian version, same concept)

All three rows share the same `parallel_group_id` (e.g. `G-001`) and have `origin=parallel`.

### Why it matters
RQ1 includes a sub-question: *does the same bias occur consistently across languages for the same concept?* Without parallel items, you can only compare EN vs FR vs BG as separate populations, not as matched pairs.
